How Korean Names Work
01
Family Name First
Korean names are written with the family name (성) first, followed by the given name. For example: Kim Taehyung (김태형) — Kim is the family name.
02
2 Syllable Given Names
Most Korean given names have 2 syllables. Each syllable (한자) carries a specific meaning, so Korean names are chosen for their meaning, not just sound.
03
Most Common Surnames
About 45% of Koreans have one of three surnames: Kim (김), Lee (이), or Park (박). Together they represent nearly half the population!
04
Meaning Matters
Korean parents carefully choose name characters for their meanings — like 지 (wisdom), 수 (excellence), 민 (cleverness), 현 (virtue).
